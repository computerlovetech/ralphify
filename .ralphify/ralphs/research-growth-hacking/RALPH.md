## What to research

### 1. Jonah Berger's "Contagious" — The 6 STEPPS

Research and apply each principle to ralphify specifically:

- **Social Currency** — How can using/sharing ralphify make people look good? What's the insider knowledge angle? How do we make people feel like they're in on something early?
- **Triggers** — What everyday moments remind developers of ralphify? What situations trigger the thought "I should use ralph for this"? How do we create environmental triggers?
- **Emotion** — What emotions does autonomous coding evoke? How do we tap into the awe of watching an agent ship code overnight? What about the anxiety of falling behind?
- **Public** — How do we make ralphify usage visible? What's the equivalent of the Apple logo on a laptop? Can we make "ralphing" a verb?
- **Practical Value** — What's the concrete, shareable value proposition? What's the "news you can use" angle? How do we package tips people want to forward?
- **Stories** — What's the Trojan horse narrative? What story carries the ralphify message inside it? "I left ralph running overnight and woke up to 47 commits" — what are the stories people will retell?

### 2. Open source growth best practices

Search for and analyze growth strategies from successful open source developer tools. Look at:

- **Launch strategies** — How did tools like Cursor, Vercel, Supabase, Tailwind, shadcn/ui, FastAPI get their first 1000 stars?
- **README optimization** — What makes a README that converts visitors to users?
- **Demo & showcase** — Screencasts, GIFs, live demos, example repos — what formats drive adoption?
- **Community seeding** — Discord vs GitHub Discussions vs Twitter/X communities. What works for dev tools in 2025-2026?
- **Developer advocacy** — Blog posts, conference talks, Twitter threads, YouTube tutorials. What content actually drives adoption?
- **Contributor experience** — Good first issues, contributing guides, fast PR reviews — how to turn users into contributors?
- **Distribution channels** — Hacker News, Reddit, Product Hunt, Twitter/X, Dev.to, newsletters. What works when and how?

### 3. Ralphify-specific opportunities

Think about what makes ralphify unique and exploitable for growth:

- The "Ralph Wiggum" meme origin — there's already cultural resonance and humor
- The simplicity — `while :; do cat PROMPT.md | claude-code ; done` as a starting point
- The visual output — watching an agent loop and commit is inherently shareable
- The harness engineering angle — riding the wave of a growing concept
- The community of people already doing "ralphing" — Geoffrey Huntley's audience

### 4. Competitive landscape

- Who else is building agent loop tooling? What's their positioning?
- How is ralphify differentiated? Where should it position itself?
- What gaps exist in the market that ralphify could own?

## Rules
- One focused research push per iteration — go deep on one angle, then refine the whole document
- Use web search extensively — find real case studies, not just theory
- Every recommendation must be specific and actionable, not generic advice
- Prioritize tactics by effort vs impact — the document should help decide what to do first
- Include examples from real open source projects, with links
- Distinguish between proven tactics and experimental ideas
- Do NOT commit or push — this is local research only
